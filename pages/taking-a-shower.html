home taking a shower

soap/shampoo/conditioner bottles  ↦  bars

My local coop sells package-free soap bars and paper-wrapped shampoo/conditioner bars. I also found a package-free shampoo bar at a farmers market. Not only did these products come with minimal packaging, they came from small local sources that I want to give my money to.

bars are better:

Solid bars last a long time, since they contain more concentrated product than bottles (which usually contain mostly water). I've been really impressed with my shampoo bars in particular; a single one often costs less than $10 and lasts over a year.

These types of bars are often made without synthetic dyes or fragrances, which feels nice and clean on many levels.

my zero waste shower stuff:

In the shower, I use the following:

  • bar of soap
  • shampoo bar
  • conditioner bar

And that is all.
